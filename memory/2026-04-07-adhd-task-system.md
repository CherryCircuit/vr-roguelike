**What you're describing is craniofacial hyperhidrosis** — excessive sweating from the head/scalp/face. It's recognized and treatable.

**Most accessible option: Glycopyrrolate topical cream (2%)**
- Applied to face/scalp, blocks sweat gland nerve signals
- 75% complete response rate in studies
- Downside: not available as pre-made wipes in Canada (Qbrexza is US-only), but a compounding pharmacy could make the cream with a prescription

**Most effective option: Botox injections to scalp/face**
- Reduces sweating by up to 87%, lasts 4-6 months
- FDA-approved for hyperhidrosis
- Requires repeat sessions, but very well-established

**Systemic option: Oral glycopyrrolate or oxybutynin**
- Pills that reduce sweating body-wide
- Side effects: dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation (dose-dependent)
- Good for severe cases

**Worth mentioning:** worth asking your doctor to check thyroid function and any medications you're on — some meds (SSRIs, SNRIs) can worsen heat intolerance and sweating.

**What it is:** A free savings card funded by pharmaceutical manufacturers that covers the price gap between brand-name drugs and their generic equivalents. You just sign up with an email and show the card (digital or physical) at the pharmacy.

- **Multi-use, never expires** — once you have it, it works indefinitely
- **Brand-name guarantee** — forces the pharmacy to dispense the actual brand instead of auto-substituting a generic
- **Instant savings at checkout** — discounts applied electronically, no rebate forms
- **Works with existing insurance** — stacks with provincial plans, group benefits, or standalone
- **100+ drugs covered** — diabetes meds, mental health, contraceptives, etc.
- **Dead simple signup** — just enter your email, get a digital card immediately

### ❌ Cons

- **Limited drug list** — only covers specific brand-name drugs from participating manufacturers. If your medication isn't on the list, it's useless
- **No help with generics** — if you're fine with generics (or already taking them), there's zero benefit
- **Pharmacy friction** — some pharmacists may not be familiar with it, incorrectly claim it's "one-time use," or forget to apply it. You may need to advocate for yourself
- **Variable savings** — the actual discount depends on your insurance and the generic price gap; it's not always the "up to 75%" they advertise
- **Manufacturer-driven** — coverage reflects pharma marketing priorities, not medical need. A drug could be dropped if the sponsor pulls out

### 🤔 Worth noting

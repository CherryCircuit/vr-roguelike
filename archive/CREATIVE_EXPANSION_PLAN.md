# CREATIVE EXPANSION PLAN
## Synthwave VR Roguelike - "Neon Voxel Chaos"

# 1. NEW WEAPONS

## MAIN WEAPONS

### **BOOMERANG DISC** 
- **Mechanic:** Throws a glowing neon frisbee that arcs outward, then returns to your hand. Hits enemies on both outbound and return path. Distance controlled by how long you hold the throw. Can curve around corners.
- **Visual:** Tron-style light disc with trailing afterimages. Satisfying *shing* sound on catch.
- **Twist:** Catching it perfectly (timing window) instantly resets cooldown. Miss the catch and it teleports back with a 2s penalty.
- **Upgrade Path:** Multi-disc (2-3 orbit you), Ricochet (bounces off walls), Homing Return (curves to hit enemies on way back)

### **GRAVITY WELL LAUNCHER**
- **Mechanic:** Fires a slow-moving purple orb that creates a gravity well on impact. Enemies get pulled toward it for 3 seconds, then it implodes dealing damage based on how many enemies it gathered.
- **Visual:** Swirling purple vortex with particle effects. Enemies stretch toward it like spaghetti.
- **Twist:** You can shoot the well to detonate it early. Works on your own projectiles too - curve shots into it!
- **Combo Potential:** Gravity well + shotgun = devastating cluster kill

### **CHAIN LIGHTNING ROD**
- **Mechanic:** Close-range cone attack. First enemy hit becomes an anchor - lightning chains to 3-5 nearby enemies. Each chain does 20% less damage but chains FASTER.
- **Visual:** Jagged neon blue/white arcs. Each chain makes a satisfying *zap* sound. Enemies glow briefly after being hit.
- **Twist:** Wet enemies (certain biomes) chain to 2x as many targets. Metal enemies (some robot types) take double damage.
- **Risk/Reward:** Must be close to use. High damage but risky positioning.

### **RICOCHET PISTOL**
- **Mechanic:** Bullets bounce off surfaces up to 5 times. Each bounce increases damage by 15%. Perfect for corridor combat.
- **Visual:** Glowing orange tracers that leave momentary light trails on bounce surfaces.
- **Twist:** Aim at the floor in front of enemies for guaranteed multi-bounce hits. Ceiling shots in tight spaces are devastating.
- **Skill Ceiling:** Expert players can bank shots around corners. Novice players still effective with direct fire.

### **VORTEX BEAM**
- **Mechanic:** Continuous beam that creates a mini black hole at the impact point. The longer you hold on a target, the stronger the pull and damage. Release to explode the accumulated energy.
- **Visual:** Purple-black beam with white-hot core. Target area warps visually. Enemies tumble toward center.
- **Twist:** Can grab small enemies and fling them at other enemies. Physics-based chaos.
- **Limitation:** High energy cost. Cannot be sustained indefinitely.

### **TIME SKIP RIFLE**
- **Mechanic:** Bullets exist in "delayed time." Fire through empty space, activate to freeze all fired bullets in place. Activate again to resume their trajectories. Set up kill zones!
- **Visual:** Bullets are translucent, ghostly. Frozen bullets pulse with blue energy.
- **Twist:** Can shoot through walls by freezing bullets, moving, then resuming. Puzzle-combat hybrid.
- **Strategic Depth:** Pre-fire before opening doors. Set up ambushes.

## ALT WEAPONS

### **DECOY HOLOGRAM**
- **Mechanic:** Deploys a holographic copy of yourself that attracts enemy attention. Lasts 8 seconds or until destroyed. 15s cooldown.
- **Visual:** Glitchy, static-covered duplicate. Flickers when damaged.
- **Twist:** When destroyed, it explodes dealing damage proportional to how many enemies were targeting it.
- **Synergy:** Deploy, then flank with shotgun. Deploy, then snipe distracted enemies.

### **TETHER HARPOON**
- **Mechanic:** Fires a tether that connects you to an enemy. You can yank them toward you, or use them as a wrecking ball to swing into other enemies.
- **Visual:** Neon green energy rope with physics simulation.
- **Twist:** Tethered enemies take damage when they collide with anything. Tethered small enemies can be used as shields (other enemies hit them).
- **Creative Use:** Tether a tank enemy, use it to clear swarms. Tether a fast enemy, let it drag you around the arena.

### **PHASE DASH**
- **Mechanic:** Instant short-range teleport in the direction you're moving. Leaves behind an explosive afterimage that detonates after 1 second.
- **Visual:** You dissolve into pixels, reassemble at destination. Afterimage is a ghostly blue shell.
- **Twist:** Can dash through enemies, damaging them. Can dash through thin walls.
- **Combo:** Dash through an enemy, they turn around, afterimage hits them in the back. Double damage.

### **SINGULARITY MINE**
- **Mechanic:** Toss a mine that creates a brief (2 second) black hole when an enemy approaches. Enemies sucked in take damage and are stunned for 1 second after release.
- **Visual:** Subtle purple glow on the mine. Activation creates a satisfying *WHUMP* sound.
- **Twist:** You can trigger your own mines by shooting them. Pre-detonate for area denial.
- **Stacking:** Multiple mines in same area create overlapping wells. Devastating but resource-expensive.

### **NANITE SWARM**
- **Mechanic:** Release a cloud of nanobots that hover in place for 10 seconds. Any enemy passing through takes damage over time and is revealed (outline visible through walls).
- **Visual:** Shimmering golden cloud, like glitter in the air. Affected enemies sparkle.
- **Twist:** Nanites can be "attracted" to your shots - bullets passing through the cloud carry some nanites with them, adding damage over time.
- **Control:** Can recall the swarm early to reduce cooldown.

### **REFLECTOR DRONE**
- **Mechanic:** A small drone orbits you for 15 seconds. It automatically reflects 50% of enemy projectiles back at them.
- **Visual:** Little hexagonal drone with a shimmering shield surface. Reflects create a *pew-pew* sound.
- **Twist:** Can "overcharge" the drone by shooting it - temporarily reflects 100% but drone takes damage and may break early.
- **Risk:** Drone has health. Tank enemies can destroy it with concentrated fire.

# 2. NEW ENEMY TYPES

## **SPIRAL SWIMMERS** (School of Fish variant)
- **Movement:** Corkscrew toward player in a tight spiral pattern. Single-voxel bodies, travel in trains of 8-12.
- **Attack:** Contact damage. Late in the train does more damage (momentum-based).
- **Weak Point:** Leading "scout" voxel - killing it scatters the school into random patterns for 3 seconds.
- **Visual:** Tiny glowing voxels in cyan/teal. Trail of light behind them like bioluminescence.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early levels: short trains, slow spiral. Late levels: long trains, tight spiral, faster, some trains split and merge.
- **Why It Works:** Hard to hit individually. Forces player to track a moving pattern. AOE weapons effective but wasteful if only one train. Distracting when combined with fast enemies.

## **GEOMETRY SHIFTERS**
- **Movement:** Tumble toward player, changing shape every 2 seconds (cube → pyramid → sphere → tetrahedron). Each shape has different properties.
- **Attack:** Contact damage varies by shape. Cube: normal. Pyramid: high damage (pointy). Sphere: low damage but bounces toward you faster. Tetrahedron: splits into 2 smaller versions on death.
- **Weak Point:** During the 0.5s transition between shapes, they take triple damage.
- **Visual:** Solid neon colors that shift with shape. Cube=red, Pyramid=orange, Sphere=yellow, Tetrahedron=pink.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: slow transitions, obvious color changes. Late: fast transitions, subtle visual cues, some shift randomly instead of predictably.
- **Why It Works:** Predictable pattern but requires timing. Creates decision points (shoot now vs wait for transition). Tetrahedron split creates chaos.

## **PULSE BOMBER**
- **Movement:** Slow, deliberate floating. Keeps medium distance from player. Teleports away when you get too close.
- **Attack:** Fires expanding rings of energy (like sonic booms). Rings travel slowly but cover wide area. Must be dodged by jumping or ducking.
- **Weak Point:** After firing, opens its core for 1.5 seconds (glows bright white). Core shot = instant kill.
- **Visual:** Dark sphere with orbiting smaller spheres. When firing, spheres align into a ring and pulse outward. Core looks like a miniature sun.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: slow rings, long cooldown, easy core access. Late: rapid-fire rings, rings can bounce off walls, core only open for 0.8s, teleports more aggressively.
- **Why It Works:** Creates vertical gameplay (jump/duck). Risk/reward for core shots. Forces movement.

## **CLONE MIMIC**
- **Movement:** Copies your last 3 seconds of movement with a 1 second delay. If you stood still, it charges.
- **Attack:** Contact damage. If it touches you, it "learns" your position and future clones will path there immediately.
- **Weak Point:** Doesn't adapt well to rapid direction changes. Moving erratically confuses it.
- **Visual:** Your character model but glitchy, static-covered, wrong colors. Leaves afterimages.
- **Twist:** Killing a clone spawns 2 smaller clones with the same behavior but less health.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: longer delay, no splitting. Late: short delay, splits 2-3 times, elite clones that predict your NEXT move based on patterns.
- **Why It Works:** Punishes repetitive movement. Rewards adaptability. Creepy visual. Strategic depth (lead clones into other enemies).

## **SPIDER WALKER**
- **Movement:** Skitters along walls, ceiling, and floor. Drops down when you pass beneath. Very fast on surfaces, slow in air.
- **Attack:** Leaps at you from walls/ceiling. On hit, latches on and does damage over time until you shake it off (move erratically or use shield).
- **Weak Point:** While on walls, glowing egg sac on abdomen. In air, no weak point but takes extra knockback.
- **Visual:** 8-legged voxel spider. Body is dark, legs are neon orange. Egg sac pulses green.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: slow skitter, predictable drops, easy to shake. Late: fast movement, drops from any angle, some explode on contact instead of latching, egg sacs spawn baby spiders on death.
- **Why It Works:** Creates 360° threat awareness. Forces vertical aiming. Baby spider spawn creates priority decisions.

## **MIRROR KNIGHT**
- **Movement:** Slow advance, but mirrors your left/right movements. You strafe left, it strafes right (toward you).
- **Attack:** Melee when close. Reflects 30% of projectile damage back at you.
- **Weak Point:** Back is unarmored. Also, if you stand perfectly still for 2 seconds, it gets confused and drops its shield (3 second window).
- **Visual:** Knight with a large reflective shield (shows your reflection). Shield is chrome, body is dark purple.
- **Twist:** Killing it shatters the shield into 3 shards that become temporary hazards on the ground.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: slow, long confusion window. Late: faster, reflects 50% damage, confusion window shorter, shield shards home toward you briefly.
- **Why It Works:** Subverts circle-strafing. Encourages stillness (rare in shooters). Creates environmental hazards on death.

## **PORTAL MANTIS**
- **Movement:** Opens a small portal in front of itself, exits from a portal near you. Cooldown on portal use. Otherwise slow walk.
- **Attack:** Melee attack with scythe arms when close. Can slash THROUGH a portal (range attack).
- **Weak Point:** For 0.5s after exiting a portal, it's disoriented and takes double damage.
- **Visual:** Mantis-like with glowing green blade arms. Portals are swirling blue/purple circles.
- **Twist:** You can shoot THROUGH its portals too. Use them to your advantage.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: long portal cooldown, obvious exit portal spawn, slow attack. Late: short cooldown, exit portal spawns behind you, rapid slashes, can chain 2-3 portals in succession.
- **Why It Works:** Disorients player. Creates tactical decision (shoot portal or enemy). Uses game's mechanics against player.

## **BLACK HOLE TOTEM**
- **Movement:** NONE. Stationary. Spawns in fixed locations (corners, elevated positions).
- **Attack:** Creates a gravity field that slowly pulls you toward it. If you touch it, instant heavy damage + stun.
- **Weak Point:** The totem itself has low health. Destroying it causes it to implode, damaging nearby enemies.
- **Visual:** Floating monolith with a swirling vortex above it. Dark with purple lightning.
- **Twist:** The gravity field affects enemies too. Can be used tactically if you're careful.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: weak pull, small radius, low health. Late: strong pull, large radius, some regenerate health if not killed quickly, elite versions that pull projectiles off course.
- **Why It Works:** Creates "no-go" zones. Tactical resource (use gravity against enemies). Changes arena flow.

## **CONDUCTOR**
- **Movement:** Slow. Always stays near other enemies.
- **Attack:** Doesn't attack directly. Links to nearby enemies with visible tethers. Linked enemies move 40% faster and take 30% less damage.
- **Weak Point:** Killing it instantly kills all linked enemies (chain overload).
- **Visual:** Robe-wearing figure with raised arms. Electric arcs connect to linked enemies. Glows bright yellow.
- **Twist:** If you damage but don't kill it, it redirects 50% of that damage to a linked enemy. Tanky enemy as a shield.
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: links 2-3 enemies, easy to spot, doesn't move. Late: links 5-6 enemies, moves to stay protected, some can link to OTHER conductors (destroying one only kills half the chain).
- **Why It Works:** Priority target. Creates enemy clusters. Risk/reward (high damage = potential waste if you don't finish it).

## **PHASE WRAITH**
- **Movement:** Exists partially out of phase. Blinks in and out of visibility. Moves fast when visible, slow when invisible.
- **Attack:** Melee only. Attacks from invisibility do double damage and ignore shields.
- **Weak Point:** While visible, it takes double damage. While invisible, it can be "pulled" into phase with EMP or energy weapons.
- **Visual:** Ghostly, translucent. Faint outline when invisible. Eyes glow red. Leaves mist trails.
- **Twist:** Killing it creates a "phase echo" - a ghost that continues its attack pattern for 5 seconds but deals no damage (distraction).
- **Difficulty Curve:** Early: long visible periods, predictable invisibility cycles. Late: short visible periods, some can stay invisible for 5+ seconds, elite versions that attack WHILE invisible (but with normal damage).
- **Why It Works:** Creates tension. Rewards observation. Forces shield discipline. Echo creates post-death chaos.

# 3. NEW BIOMES

## **THE STACK** (Difficulty: Medium)
- **Theme:** Brutalist architecture meets synthwave. Endless stacked platforms and brutal concrete structures with neon graffiti.
- **Color Palette:** Concrete gray, hot pink graffiti, cyan accent lights, deep shadows.
- **Unique Enemy:** **LED GRAFFITI** - Enemies that blend into the graffiti, only visible when they move or by their faint glow.
- **Unique Hazard:** Moving platforms. Some platforms rotate, some drop after standing on them for 2 seconds. Gaps require jumping.
- **Environmental Feature:** Graffiti tags are actually hints. Tags that say "DROP" indicate safe dropping points. Tags that say "VOID" indicate instant-kill pits.
- **Difficulty Twist:** Vertical combat emphasized. Enemies can be above or below you. Limited visibility in deep shadows.

## **OCEAN FLOOR** (Difficulty: Easy-Medium)
- **Theme:** Underwater synthwave. Coral reefs made of neon, schools of digital fish, caustic light patterns.
- **Color Palette:** Deep blue-black, bioluminescent greens and teals, purple coral, white sand.
- **Unique Enemy:** **CURRENT DRIFTER** - Passive-aggressive enemy that floats with the current. Moves toward you when you attack anything nearby (attracted to violence).
- **Unique Hazard:** Currents. Push you in fixed directions. Some areas have strong updrafts or downdrafts. Oxygen is not a concern but visibility is reduced at range.
- **Environmental Feature:** Coral provides cover but can be destroyed. Fish schools are harmless but can obscure vision. Some coral pulses with light that reveals invisible enemies.
- **Difficulty Twist:** Movement is slower but more fluid. Projectiles travel slower. Melee enemies are more dangerous due to slower retreat.

## **DIGITAL RAIN** (Difficulty: Hard)
- **Theme:** Matrix-style data rain. Vertical green characters falling constantly. Everything is data. Glitch effects.
- **Color Palette:** Matrix green, black, white data artifacts, occasional red glitch zones.
- **Unique Enemy:** **CORRUPTED FILE** - Enemies that "glitch" - teleport short distances randomly, sometimes split into two weaker versions, sometimes merge with other Corrupted Files.
- **Unique Hazard:** Glitch zones. Red-tinted areas where your weapons behave unpredictably (projectiles curve, cooldowns vary, damage fluctuates).
- **Environmental Feature:** The rain is cosmetic but can be "read" - patterns in the rain indicate enemy spawns 3 seconds before they appear. Skilled players can predict spawns.
- **Difficulty Twist:** High unpredictability. Good runs feel amazing, bad runs feel unfair. Rewards pattern recognition and adaptability.

## **VAPOR SUNSET** (Difficulty: Easy)
- **Theme:** Classic vaporwave. Endless beach at sunset. Palm trees, grid on the water, nostalgic sadness.
- **Color Palette:** Pink sky, orange sun, purple water, teal grid lines, black palm silhouettes.
- **Unique Enemy:** **STATUE DRIFT** - Posed, statue-like enemies that only move when you're not looking at them (Weeping Angel style). Very slow, but always closing in.
- **Unique Hazard:** None. This is a "breather" biome.
- **Environmental Feature:** Palm trees provide cover. The sun sets slowly over the course of the biome (5 levels), getting darker each level.
- **Difficulty Twist:** Psychological horror element. Statue Drift aren't dangerous if you keep track, but in later levels there are more of them and faster. Tension without high difficulty.

## **CIRCUIT BOARD** (Difficulty: Medium-Hard)
- **Theme:** Micro-scale. You're tiny, running across a massive circuit board. Capacitors are towers, traces are paths.
- **Color Palette:** Green PCB, gold traces, silver solder points, red power LEDs, black IC chips.
- **Unique Enemy:** **ELECTRON FLOW** - Fast-moving energy enemies that follow the gold traces. Predictable paths but very fast. "Traffic" of enemies.
- **Unique Hazard:** Power zones. Glowing red areas near capacitors deal damage over time. Capacitors discharge periodically (AoE damage).
- **Environmental Feature:** Traces guide movement (some are one-way due to diodes). IC chips are buildings you can enter (indoor combat). Solder points are elevated positions.
- **Difficulty Twist:** Enemy movement is predictable but fast. "Highway" traces have constant enemy traffic. Strategic positioning matters more than aim.

## **KALEIDOSCOPE** (Difficulty: Very Hard)
- **Theme:** Infinite mirror maze. Geometric patterns shift constantly. Reality folds in on itself.
- **Color Palette:** Every color, but arranged in symmetric patterns. High saturation. White "fold" lines.
- **Unique Enemy:** **MIRROR SHARD** - Enemies that create mirror images of themselves. 1-3 fake copies. Only one takes damage, others pass through you harmlessly but create visual chaos.
- **Unique Hazard:** Reality folds. Every 30 seconds, the level "folds" - your position is mirrored, teleported, or rotated. Disorienting.
- **Environmental Feature:** Mirrors show enemy positions you can't see directly. Some mirrors are portals. Some mirrors are fake (just textures).
- **Difficulty Twist:** Extreme disorientation. Not for beginners. Requires spatial memory and calm under pressure. High skill ceiling.

## **RETRO ARCADE** (Difficulty: Medium)
- **Theme:** Inside an 80s arcade. Neon marquees, carpet patterns, rows of arcade cabinets.
- **Color Palette:** Black light aesthetic. Neon purples, blues, pinks. Carpet is geometric patterns in dark colors.
- **Unique Enemy:** **PIXEL GHOST** - 8-bit style enemies that move in grid patterns. Predictable but can "eat" power pellets in the environment to become aggressive.
- **Unique Hazard:** Arcade cabinets turn on and off. When on, they spawn enemies. When off, they provide cover. Can be destroyed but take significant damage.
- **Environmental Feature:** Some arcade cabinets are playable (interact to get temporary power-ups). Carpet patterns indicate safe paths (no enemy spawns on certain patterns).
- **Difficulty Twist:** Resource management. Destroying cabinets reduces spawn points but uses ammo. Power-ups are tempting but require time.

## **NEON RAINFOREST** (Difficulty: Medium-Hard)
- **Theme:** Jungle but cyberpunk. Glowing plants, holographic butterflies, bioluminescent everything.
- **Color Palette:** Deep greens, electric blues, glowing pinks, yellow pollen, black shadows.
- **Unique Enemy:** **VINE STRANGLER** - Camouflaged until it attacks. Wraps around pillars/trees. Snares you if you pass close. Must shoot to break free.
- **Unique Hazard:** Pollen clouds. Reduce visibility. Some heal enemies, some slow you, some damage you. Color-coded (pink=heal, blue=slow, yellow=damage).
- **Environmental Feature:** Massive leaves provide mobile cover (they move in wind). Rivers of glowing liquid flow through - fast travel but you can't shoot while in them. Some plants explode when shot (environmental grenades).
- **Difficulty Twist:** Visibility management. Dense foliage blocks line of sight. Enemies can hide easily. Explosive plants create risk/reward for missed shots.

## **VOID GARDEN** (Difficulty: Hard)
- **Theme:** Space garden. Floating platforms in the void. Crystalline plants. Stars and nebulae in background.
- **Color Palette:** Black void, white stars, crystal purples/blues, gold plants, silver platforms.
- **Unique Enemy:** **VOID FISH** - Swim through the void. Can move through platforms. Attack from below or above. Only visible when backlit by stars.
- **Unique Hazard:** No floor. Fall off a platform, instant death. Platforms are small and sparse. Void pulls you slightly toward the center (always fighting drift).
- **Environmental Feature:** Gravity is lower. Jumps are higher, floating is possible for short periods. Some plants grow and create new platforms over time. Crystals can be shot to create temporary barriers.
- **Difficulty Twist:** Positioning is everything. Knockback is dangerous. Void Fish attack from unexpected angles. Low gravity changes combat feel significantly.

## **SUNRISE HIGHWAY** (Difficulty: Easy)
- **Theme:** Endless highway at dawn. Retro-futuristic cars in the distance. Open road.
- **Color Palette:** Orange/pink sunrise, gray road, white lines, distant city silhouettes in purple.
- **Unique Enemy:** **ROAD RAGER** - Fast enemies that charge in straight lines. Predictable but very fast. Telegraph their charges with honk sounds.
- **Unique Hazard:** Cars. Occasional cars drive through on the highway lanes. Deal massive damage to anything they hit (including enemies). Can be dodged or used tactically.
- **Environmental Feature:** Lane markers indicate safe zones from cars. Overpasses provide elevated positions. Some areas have construction barriers (cover).
- **Difficulty Twist:** Open sight lines favor ranged weapons. Road Ragers are easy to predict but require quick reflexes. Cars can turn a bad situation around (lure enemies into traffic).

# 4. ENEMY PHYSICS DEATH EFFECTS

## Core Design Philosophy
The death effect should be **satisfying, readable, and performant**. Each death is a micro-reward. The player should feel impact.

## Voxel Separation System

### **DEATH TRIGGER**
When enemy health reaches 0:
1. Disable enemy AI and collision
2. Calculate voxel count (based on enemy type)
3. Spawn voxel debris objects (from pool)
4. Apply physics forces to each voxel
5. Begin disintegration timer

### **VOXEL SEPARATION LOGIC**

**Force Application:**
- **Explosive Force:** Random direction, magnitude based on weapon damage (overkill = bigger explosion)
- **Inherited Velocity:** Voxels retain 30% of enemy's movement velocity at death
- **Gravity:** Reduced gravity (0.3x normal) for "floaty" aesthetic
- **Spin:** Each voxel gets random rotation speed (5-15 rad/s)

**Bounce Physics:**
- **Floor Collision:** Coefficient of restitution = 0.5 (moderate bounce)
- **Wall Collision:** COR = 0.3 (less bounce)
- **Voxel-Voxel:** Collision disabled (too expensive)
- **Max Bounces:** 3 (after 3rd bounce, voxels stop horizontal movement)

### **DISINTEGRATION EFFECTS**

**Visual Disintegration:**
- Voxel alpha decreases over time (1.5-3 seconds based on enemy type)
- Voxel scale decreases (shrink effect)
- Color shifts to white as final disintegration
- Particle trail (smaller pixels) as voxels shrink

**Performance Optimization:**
- After 0.5 seconds, voxels that have bounced 3+ times are marked "dead"
- Dead voxels stop physics simulation, only animate disintegration
- Object pooling: 50-100 voxel debris objects recycled per frame
- Distance culling: voxels far from camera receive simplified physics

### **ENEMY TYPE VARIATIONS**

**Basic Enemy:**
- 8-12 voxels
- Standard explosion force
- Blue/cyan color fade to white
- 1.5 second disintegration

**Fast Enemy:**
- 4-6 voxels (smaller)
- High explosive force (speed translates to explosion)
- Yellow/orange fade to white
- 1.0 second disintegration (faster)
- Leaves speed-trail particles

**Tank Enemy:**
- 20-30 voxels
- Low explosive force (heavy)
- Deep purple fade to white
- 3.0 second disintegration (slow)
- Voxels are larger (2x scale)
- Some voxels may remain as temporary cover (5 seconds)

**Swarm Enemy:**
- 2-3 voxels (tiny)
- Chaotic random force directions
- Pink fade to white
- 0.8 second disintegration (very fast)
- High pitch "pop" sound

**Spiral Swimmer:**
- Each voxel in the train becomes individual debris
- Forces follow the spiral pattern (maintains shape briefly)
- Teal fade to white
- 1.2 second disintegration
- Creates a beautiful spiral explosion

**Geometry Shifter:**
- Voxels separate according to current shape
- Shape-specific disintegration:
  - Cube: expands outward
  - Pyramid: apex voxels fly highest
  - Sphere: uniform expansion
  - Tetrahedron: splits into mini-tetrahedrons before disintegrating
- Color-specific fade (matches shape color)
- 2.0 second disintegration

**Pulse Bomber:**
- Core voxels (white) have highest force (mini explosion)
- Orb voxels (dark) spiral outward
- Creates ring pattern on floor from bouncing orbs
- 2.5 second disintegration
- Bouncing orbs leave light trails

**Portal Mantis:**
- Voxels separate, then half are pulled into a mini-portal (visual only)
- Remaining voxels disintegrate normally
- Portal closes with a visual "snap"
- 1.8 second disintegration
- Creates sense of being pulled away

### **SPECIAL DEATH EFFECTS**

**Critical Kill (weak point):**
- Voxel explosion force +50%
- Gold particle burst
- Satisfying "crack" sound
- Voxel trails are gold instead of white

**Overkill (damage > remaining HP by 3x):**
- Voxel explosion force +100%
- Screen shake (minor)
- Voxel count appears to double (visual trick, actually spawn extra particles)
- Loud explosive sound

**Chain Kill (multiple enemies within 0.5s):**
- Each subsequent death gets +20% force
- Combo counter appears (x2, x3, etc.)
- Sound pitch increases with combo
- Creates "rhythm" feeling

**Elemental Death:**
- Fire weapon: voxels burn (orange particles, faster disintegration)
- Ice weapon: voxels shatter (break into smaller voxels, no bounce)
- Electric weapon: voxels arc to nearby enemies (mini chain lightning)
- Gravity weapon: voxels collapse inward before disappearing

### **PERFORMANCE SAFEGUARDS**

**Maximum Active Voxel Debris:**
- Hard cap: 200 active physics voxels
- When cap reached, oldest voxels disintegrate instantly
- Visual only (no physics) voxels don't count toward cap

**Level of Detail:**
- Close voxels: Full physics, full particles
- Medium voxels: Simplified physics, no particles
- Far voxels: No physics (drift only), no particles

**Frame Rate Protection:**
- If FPS drops below 45, reduce max voxel cap to 100
- If FPS drops below 30, reduce to 50
- Gradual restoration as FPS improves

**Object Pooling:**
- Pre-allocate voxel debris pool (300 objects)
- Pre-allocate particle pool (500 particles)
- No runtime allocation during gameplay

# 5. RUN VARIETY SYSTEM

## Design Goals
1. Each run should feel different
2. Not all content should be seen in a single run
3. Replay value comes from seeing new combinations
4. Some runs should be "easier" (new player friendly)
5. Some runs should be "harder" (veteran challenge)

## The "Seed Deck" System

### **CONCEPT**
Think of each run as drawing from shuffled decks of cards. Each deck contains a subset of the total content. You see what you drew, not everything.

### **DECKS**

**Enemy Deck:**
- Total enemy types: 14 (4 existing + 10 new)
- Enemy deck size: 8-10 (not all enemies appear in a run)
- Deck composition:
  - Core enemies (always included): Basic, Fast (2 types)
  - Deck enemies (randomly selected): 6-8 from remaining 12 types
  - Biome-specific enemies (added when entering that biome): 1-2 types

**Biome Deck:**
- Total biomes: 15 (5 existing + 10 new)
- Biome deck size: 5-7 (a run is ~25 levels, 5 levels per biome)
- Deck composition:
  - Starting biome: Always "Sunrise Highway" or "Vapor Sunset" (easy start)
  - Middle biomes: 3-4 random from medium difficulty pool
  - Late biome: 1-2 from hard difficulty pool
  - Final biome: Always the "true final" (existing final biome)

**Weapon Deck:**
- Total weapons: 24 (12 existing + 12 new)
- Weapon deck size: 15-18 (most weapons available, but not all)
- Deck composition:
  - Starting weapons: 3 random from easy-to-use pool
  - Unlockable weapons: 10-12 from remaining pool
  - Exotic weapons: 2-3 from "rare" pool (only appear in late shops)

### **SEED GENERATION**

**Run Seed:**
- Generated at run start
- 32-bit integer
- Determines all deck compositions and order
- Can be manually entered for "challenge runs" or shared seeds

**Deck Shuffling:**
- Enemy deck: Shuffled, revealed progressively (you don't know what's coming until it spawns)
- Biome deck: Shuffled, next biome revealed 1 level before transition
- Weapon deck: Shuffled, shop inventory draws from deck

### **DIFFICULTY MODIFIERS**

**Run Difficulty Tiers:**

**Tier 1 - "Chill Run" (20% of runs)**
- Enemy deck: Easier composition (no hard enemies)
- Biome deck: Easier composition (Vapor Sunset, Sunrise Highway, Ocean Floor)
- More health pickups
- Fewer elite enemies
- Intended for: New players, warm-up runs

**Tier 2 - "Standard Run" (60% of runs)**
- Enemy deck: Balanced mix
- Biome deck: Balanced progression
- Normal pickup rates
- Normal elite rates
- Intended for: Regular gameplay

**Tier 3 - "Challenge Run" (20% of runs)**
- Enemy deck: Hard composition (more hard enemies, fewer easy ones)
- Biome deck: Hard composition (Kaleidoscope, Void Garden, Digital Rain)
- Fewer health pickups
- More elite enemies
- Intended for: Veterans, high scores

**Tier Selection:**
- Tier 1: 0-2 previous wins
- Tier 2: 3-10 previous wins
- Tier 3: 11+ previous wins
- Can be overridden in settings ("Always Standard", "Always Challenge", etc.)

### **BIOME ENEMY SPAWN RULES**

**Spawning Algorithm:**
```
For each level:
  1. Draw from Enemy Deck (3-5 enemy types active this level)
  2. Apply Biome Modifiers (biome-specific enemies added/removed)
  3. Apply Level Modifiers (later levels = more enemies, more elites)
  4. Apply Run Modifiers (challenge run = +20% enemy count)
```

**Biome Enemy Affinities:**
- Each biome has "preferred" enemy types (more likely to spawn)
- Each biome has "excluded" enemy types (never spawn)
- Creates thematic consistency

Example:
- **Ocean Floor:** Prefers Spiral Swimmer, Current Drifter. Excludes Spider Walker, Road Rager.
- **Void Garden:** Prefers Void Fish, Phase Wraith. Excludes Road Rager, School of Fish.
- **Circuit Board:** Prefers Electron Flow, Conductor. Excludes Vine Strangler, Statue Drift.

### **WEAPON SHOP SYSTEM**

**Shop Inventory:**
- Each shop visit draws 4-6 items from Weapon Deck
- Items already purchased are removed from deck (no duplicates)
- Late-game shops have higher chance of rare weapons
- Some weapons only appear in specific biomes (thematic)

**Weapon Rarity:**
- **Common:** 60% of deck, available early
- **Uncommon:** 25% of deck, available mid
- **Rare:** 12% of deck, available late
- **Legendary:** 3% of deck, only in final biome shops

**Daily/Weekly Seeds:**
- Same seed for all players (global challenge)
- Leaderboards for daily/weekly runs
- Creates community competition

### **RUN VARIETY METRICS**

**Tracking (per run):**
- Enemies encountered (new vs. seen before)
- Biomes visited (new vs. seen before)
- Weapons used (new vs. seen before)
- Unique combinations (enemy + biome pairings)

**Goal:**
- First 5 runs: 40-60% new content per run
- Runs 6-20: 15-25% new content per run
- Runs 20+: 5-10% new content per run (mostly seen everything, but new combinations)

**Avoid:**
- Runs where 0% is new (stale)
- Runs where 100% is new (overwhelming)
- Identical runs (same seed should be rare)

### **META-PROGRESSION (OPTIONAL)**

**Unlock System:**
- Completing runs unlocks new content for the deck pool
- Example: Beat "Ocean Floor" → unlocks "Current Drifter" for future runs
- Creates sense of progression without making runs easier (just more varied)

**Unlock Tiers:**
- Tier 1 unlocks: Complete 1 run
- Tier 2 unlocks: Complete 3 runs
- Tier 3 unlocks: Complete 10 runs
- Secret unlocks: Hidden conditions (beat run with only melee weapons, etc.)
